# Reference: Tactical discovery / sales call script

Use for: paid-ad / inbound discovery calls (fixed-length slots, self-booked). For warm intros / referrals, swap to
`discovery-methodology.md`.

The script is a structure, not a teleprompter. Follow the sequence; phrase in your own voice.

---

## INTRO & FRAME (<= 4 min)

Hey [Name], how's it going?

[Short rapport, 10-20 seconds max] -> I see you're calling from [Place - research you've done, something about it].
Are you there at the moment?

For context, my name is **[Name]**. I'm the [founder / growth consultant] of [your company].

I work with our biggest clients on helping them [dream outcome].

We've got about [call length] today - is it okay if we jump right in?

*[wait for confirmation]*

Today, my goal is pretty simple:

- Really understand where you're at right now
- Get clear on where you want to go
- And then, if I'm confident we can help, I'll walk you through how we'd do that and what it would look like to work
  together.

If it's not a fit, I'll tell you that too. Does that sound fair?

---

## DISCOVERY

### Opening dig (why are you here, really)

I know you found out about us through our ads - what made you interested to book the time with me today for this
conversation?

[Dig 1-2 times: "Tell me more about that." "How long has that been going on?"]

### HIGH-LEVEL STORY

Got it. And how did you get into this business?

*[Short answer only - don't let them ramble 10 minutes.]*

### METRICS (rapid-fire - get permission, then go)

Cool, I'm going to rapid-fire a few questions just to get a clear picture of the business right now and see if it's a
fit. That work?

*[Wait for yes.]*

- How many people have you got? -> *[RECAP]*
- And roughly what's the annual turnover? -> *[RECAP]*
- What's your profit margin on that? -> *[RECAP]*
- Is the business growing, flat, or declining YoY?
  - Growing -> Amazing, what's driving the growth?
  - Flat / Declining -> Why do you think it is?
- What's your revenue goal for this / next year?

**Recap aloud:**

> Got it, so right now you're at about **[REVENUE + PROFIT]**, the business is **[GROWING / FLAT / DECLINING]** YoY,
> operating with a team of **[EMPLOYEES]**, and the goal is to grow to **[REVENUE GOAL]**. Perfect - that gives me a
> clear picture numbers-wise.

### PROBING QUESTIONS - current state

Pick 3-5 of these based on which constraints you anticipated in the brief. Don't fire all of them.

**As the business has grown, has profitability kept pace - or has it been more of a case where you're busier but not
necessarily better off?**
-> Is it the case that you need to linearly hire more people as the business grows?
*Listening for: margins flat / declining despite revenue growth, cost creep, no visibility into what's profitable.*
-> **Constraint #2**

---

**Walk me through what a typical day actually looks like for you.**
-> What ratio is you working in the business vs. on the business?
*Listening for: firefighting, wearing too many hats, reactive mode, dependence on specific people, no systems
catching things before they break.* -> **Constraint #1**

---

**If you could wave a magic wand and next month you jump to [REVENUE GOAL], what would break operationally?**
-> Aside from [what they just said], what else would become a constraint?

*This is the most important question on the call.* It forces them to reveal their real constraints through the lens of
growth - not as complaints about today, but as the things capping their future. Whatever they say first is the
loudest constraint. Pull on that thread before moving on.

---

**Have you implemented any AI solutions in your business / team?**
-> **YES**: Do you feel the business is actually more productive because of it - or have people just gotten faster at
their own work?
-> **NO**: Are you aware if your team uses any AI assistant tools during their job?
*Listening for: individuals using tools with no company strategy, owner aware but unsure where to start, no mechanism
to capture gains.* -> **Constraint #3**

---

**What systems do you and your team use on a regular basis?** (ERP, CRM, anything they rely on day-to-day)
-> Do those systems talk to each other - or does someone have to manually move information between them? Errors or
things falling through the cracks?
*Listening for: multiple disconnected platforms, copy-pasting, manual data entry, workarounds gone permanent.* ->
infrastructure underneath **#1 and #4**

---

**How much of your team's work is prep, admin, manual work - stuff that's necessary but doesn't actually provide value
to the company?**
-> Is there anyone where that's basically their whole role? Would you need another person like that if you grow?
*Listening for: full-time admin roles, skilled people doing process work, same data entered multiple times.* ->
**Constraint #4** (also: this is where the quantification number comes from)

---

**When someone on your team needs an answer - a customer question, a process detail, understanding what's happening
elsewhere in the business - how do they get it? Is there a system, or is it usually a person?**
-> How long does it take to get someone new up to speed? What does that process look like?
*Listening for: "they ask me," "they ask [specific person]," tribal knowledge, training = shadowing, decisions
delayed because the person who knows is unavailable.* -> **Constraint #5**

---

## DESIRED STATE

Now, looking ahead a bit...

- What are the main areas you want to improve in the business this year? *[Let them talk.]*
- Outside of [what they said], what else are you trying to change? *(here you can suggest something you feel they
  need)* *[Let them talk.]*
- Let's say we leave this call today, never speak again, and I bump into you a year from now. If everything went the
  way you want and all these problems disappeared, what would the business look like? *[Let them talk.]*

## PAIN & CONSTRAINTS

Got it. Given where you are now and where you want to go...

Pick a couple - not both:

- What have you already tried to fix this?
- Why do you think it hasn't worked yet?

*[Let them talk.]*

## URGENCY (cost of inaction)

If nothing really changes and you're still dealing with the same problems 6-12 months from now... what does that mean
for you?

*[Pause. Let them answer fully. Push for a money number or specific consequence - "what does that cost?" "how many
deals would you miss?" "how many people would you have to hire?"]*

---

## DIAGNOSIS (state the constraints back)

Okay, I think I've got a pretty clear picture.

The way we're able to help businesses like yours solve operational constraints and increase profitability is we
obsess over two things: **focus** and **leverage**.

- **Focus** - we deal only with the few biggest constraints first. If you can double your business by doing one
  thing, why would you do ten...
- **Leverage** - implementing automation, AI, and better systems so you get there with less time, money, and effort.

Big picture, it sounds like the two main things holding you back right now are:

1. **[Constraint from the constraint library]**, and
2. **[Constraint from the library]**.

Does that feel accurate?

*[Let them confirm.]*

And you told me you want to be at **[GOAL]** with **[DREAM STATE]**. That's not going to happen with the current
setup.

What we do is help fix exactly those constraints using focus and leverage so you can make that jump.

To do that properly, I want to get really specific about where to start. Sound good?

---

## WORKFLOW IDENTIFICATION (narrow constraint -> workflow -> success number)

Purpose: by the end, you can brief the team to build a working demo for Call 2. **If you finish this section without a
clear workflow, the demo will not land.**

### Step 1 - Constraint -> Function

Of those two constraints, which one is costing you more right now - [Constraint 1] or [Constraint 2]?

Got it. When you think about [the constraint they picked], where in the business does it show up the most?

*[Listen for a function: finance, operations, customer service, sales, fulfilment, HR. If they say "across the whole
business" -> "If you had to pick one area where it hurts the most, where would you start?"]*

### Step 2 - Function -> Specific workflow

Inside [function they named], walk me through one specific process that - if it ran completely differently - would
change a lot for you.

*[They'll describe 2-3 processes. Then:]* Of those, which one would you fix first?

Walk me through what happens today, step by step. Who does what, in what order, using what.

### Step 3 - Systems map

- What systems does that workflow touch from start to finish?
- Where does the data start? Where does it end up?
- Is anything in that flow done outside a system - paper forms, phone calls, anything not in software? *(real friction
  usually lives here)*

### Step 4 - Quantify the cost

- How many people touch this workflow in a typical week?
- Roughly how many hours total across all of them? *(if they don't know: "If you had to guess - is it more like 5
  hours a week, 20, or 50?")*
- How often does something go wrong with it - errors, things falling through, re-work?
- **If you doubled tomorrow, would this workflow break, or would you just hire another person to do more of it?**
  *(this question separates a true constraint from busywork - if they'd hire, it's a true constraint)*

### Step 5 - Set the success bar

If we built something that did this for you, what would the result need to look like for you to call it a clear win?

*[Push for a number:]*
-> "What number would tell you it worked?"
-> "If we save you 5 hours a week, is that a win? 10? 20?"
-> "If we cut the error rate from where it is to half, is that enough?"

**The number they give is the success threshold for the pilot. Write it down.** This gets agreed in writing in Phase
1.

Confirm: "So if we built something that hit [their number], that would be a clear win for you."

### Step 6 - Tie it back to the business picture

Recap aloud:

> When we started, you told me the business is at **[REVENUE]** with **[PROFIT MARGIN]**, growing toward **[REVENUE
> GOAL]**, and the dream state looks like **[DREAM STATE]**.
>
> The two main things holding you back are **[CONSTRAINT 1]** and **[CONSTRAINT 2]**, and of those, **[CHOSEN]** is
> hurting you most.
>
> Inside that, the specific workflow that - if it ran differently - would change the most is **[WORKFLOW]**. That
> workflow currently takes **[X HOURS / PEOPLE / COST]**, goes wrong **[FREQUENCY]**, and the win you'd want is
> **[SUCCESS NUMBER]**.
>
> That's what we'd start with.

Then frame scope:

> We're not going to fix everything in one go. We start with the one workflow that gives the biggest, most measurable
> win - get it working, prove it, then use that as the foundation for the next.
>
> If we hit **[SUCCESS NUMBER]** on **[WORKFLOW]**, it won't fix **[CONSTRAINT]** entirely. But it'll be the first
> real dent, and it'll prove out the model so we know exactly how to take on the next piece.

Confirm. If they push back that scope is too narrow ("I need more than one workflow"), that's a **buying signal**, not
a scope objection:

> "That's exactly why we structure it this way. The first workflow is the proof. Once it's working, the second ships
> in a fraction of the time because the foundation is already there."

---

## CLOSE - BOOK THE DEMO

Perfect. So here's what I'd like to do.

Rather than send you a proposal document and have you imagine what this could look like, what I'll do is go away, talk
it through with my team, and actually build you a working version of what we'd deploy for [the workflow they named].

Then we jump back on a call - ideally tomorrow or the day after - I'll walk you through it, you'll see exactly what it
does, and you can tell me whether it's the right direction or not.

By the end of that second call, you'll have a clear yes or no on whether to move forward, and we both avoid weeks of
back-and-forth on something that might not even be what you need.

How does that sound?

*[Wait for confirmation.]*

I've got [time slot tomorrow] or [time slot day after] - which works better?

**[Lock the calendar invite live on the call. Confirm they've got it.]**

One more thing - given how specific the demo is going to be, is there anyone else on your side who'd want to see it?
Operations director, CFO, anyone you'd want in the room when we walk through it?

*[If yes: get their email. Add to the invite. This is the stakeholder pre-handle - stakeholders see the demo with you,
not a description of it from you afterwards.]*

Perfect. I'll get started and I'll see you [day / time]. If anything comes up, you've got my email. Thanks for your
time today, [Name].

# DEMO / SALES CALL

### FRAME

> "Myself and the team spent a couple of hours preparing this walkthrough for you. Its purpose is to show you how we
> would approach solving [challenge] and [getting to dream outcome] based on the information you shared with me in our
> conversations so far. Then, if you would like, I can show you what working together would look like on building this
> together. Does that sound good?"

> "Great! Before I show you what we prepared for today, I want to recap what we discussed so I make sure I have the
> right picture and have everything I need in case anything's changed since we last spoke. Works for you?"

> "So... [Recap]..."

### DEMO

Same call as discovery if calendar allows; otherwise next day at the latest. **Every day between discovery and demo
is a day the prospect talks themselves out of it.**

What the demo is:

- A live, clickable version of the solution built for the specific workflow they named in discovery.
- Not a screen recording. Not a slide. Something they can interact with on the call.
- Either pulled from your existing bank if there's a close match, or spun up in 15-30 min between calls. The prospect
  doesn't need to know which.

What the demo needs to show:

- Their own workflow inside it. Reference the specific workflow they described, the systems they named, and ideally
  the success number they gave you.
- If it feels generic, it will not close.

### FIT CHECK

- On a scale of 1-10, how aligned does this sound with what you're actually trying to solve?
- What makes it a [THEIR NUMBER] and not a lower number?
- If we did this, when would you realistically want to start working on it - now, or later in the year?

**[STOP SCREEN SHARE]**

*Target: reach price by minute 30-35.*

### CLOSE

Okay, cool. So, is this something you want to move forward with then?

*[If yes, continue. If no, objection-handle until they say yes.]*

Perfect! Typically we finalise everything on this call with people who want to work with us, since we only take on a
certain number of new builds at a time and people want to secure their build.

Is that something you want to do?

*[Shut up. Let them talk.]*

*[If yes -> use the Pilot Proposal Structure.]*

### HANDLING PAYMENT DETAILS

Perfect. I've just dropped a secure payment link in the chat. Can you see it?

*[Wait for yes.]*

Go ahead and click it - it'll open the checkout. I'll start sorting onboarding on my side while you do that, so we can
hit the ground running. Just let me know once it goes through or if anything looks off.

*[Wait. Once they confirm / you see payment:]*

Perfect, I see that on my end too, thank you. Let me walk you through what happens next and how onboarding works from
here.

**If they say they'll do it later:**

> Totally get that. The only reason I suggest we do it now is that slots for the workshops are limited and we
> frequently get booked out for months. I can secure a spot you prefer for you right now instead of you having to go
> through links later and maybe wait if it fills up.
> Would you be open to just knocking it out now while we're both here? It should only take a minute.

**If they really can't:** ask them to confirm when they will do it.

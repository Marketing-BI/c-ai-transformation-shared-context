# Reference: Open-ended discovery methodology

A conversational discovery approach for sales calls. Use for: warm intros / referral calls (where the script's
rapid-fire metrics structure would feel transactional), and as the underlying phrasing layer for any sales call. The
script (`call-script.md`) is the **structure**; this is the **voice**.

---

## Core principle

**Open-ended discovery in conversational order, not interview-style.**

The script's rapid-fire metrics block ("how many people, what's turnover, what's profit margin") is appropriate for
paid-ad / inbound where the prospect knows it's a sales call and expects qualification. For warm intros, lead with
story and let the metrics surface through the conversation - don't grill.

---

## Phrasing library

Use these phrasings inside any mode when you want a question to land softer than the script's direct version.

- **"If you could wave a magic wand"** - get the prospect to envision a desired future state
  > "If you could wave a magic wand and one thing in the business worked better than it does today, what would it be?"

- **"Desired state"** - name what better looks like
  > "What does 'better' look like for you 12 months from now?"

- **"Cost of inaction"** - quantify what NOT solving costs (the script's URGENCY section, but softer)
  > "If nothing changes - same systems, same processes - what does that cost you over the next year? In money, in
  > time, in deals you can't service?"

- **"Why now"** - the deepest probe, layered
  > Layer 1: "Why is this conversation happening now vs. six months ago or six months from now?"
  > Layer 2: "What changed?"
  > Layer 3: "And how is that landing internally?"

---

## What this approach pushes back on

- **Pitching before discovering.** Even one sentence about the offer before the prospect has talked is too early.
- **Listing capabilities.** Capabilities don't sell. The constraint they want to fix sells.
- **Symmetrical small-talk.** First 3-5 minutes max. Then move.
- **Answering your own question.** Three full seconds of silence is fine. Five is even better.
- **Deflecting their question.** If they ask "what do you actually do" - answer it in one clean sentence, then bridge
  back: "but more useful than me describing it - what's the headache you'd want to test it against?"

---

## What this approach treats as foundational (don't compromise on these)

1. **Capture qualification before exiting discovery** - budget / team size / revenue / decision-making authority. All
   four.
2. **Push "why now" at least 2 layers deep.** Surface answers are surface.
3. **Quantify pain in money.** Abstract pain doesn't close.
4. **Anchor an investment range before the call ends.** Even directional. Don't leave price gravity floating.
5. **30-second test.** If you can't explain the prospect's business in 30 seconds after the call -> book a 2nd
   discovery, don't push to a decision-maker intro.

---

## When the script vs. open-ended discovery

| Call type | Lead with |
|-----------|-----------|
| Paid-ad / inbound (self-booked, fixed-length slot) | **Script** (structure) + open-ended (phrasing) |
| Warm intro from a referral / partner | **Open-ended** + the constraint library as the diagnostic |
| Outbound cold call (rare) | **Script** (you need structure when there's no warmth) |
| 2nd discovery after a weak 1st | **Script** WORKFLOW IDENTIFICATION section, drilled hard |
| Demo / sales call (Call 2) | **Script DEMO section** - frame, demo, fit check, close |

When in doubt: the script for the structure of what to cover, open-ended discovery for how to phrase it.

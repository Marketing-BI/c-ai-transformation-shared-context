# Reference: Constraints library

A library of the recurring operational constraints that discovery calls tend to surface for AI / automation /
data-modernization offers. Use for: anticipating which constraints will surface in a call (PRE mode), labelling what
you heard back to the prospect (during DIAGNOSIS), scoring whether the call landed on a constraint by name (POST
mode).

Every paid-ad / inbound discovery call should end with **two of these named back to the prospect** as the diagnosis.
If you exit the call without naming two of them, the diagnosis didn't land. Adapt or extend this library to the
team's own offer - it is a starting point, not a fixed taxonomy.

---

## #1 - The day-to-day of the business feels chaotic

**How it shows up** (listen for):

- "Priorities shift daily - we're always reacting."
- "Everything's urgent, so we never get ahead."
- "Key people are bottlenecks - if they're not available, things stop."
- "We meet deadlines, but only by grinding."
- "Feels like constant firefighting."
- "No breathing room - just one crisis after another."

**How to say it back:**

> "It sounds like priorities shift daily, key people become bottlenecks, and you're meeting deadlines through effort
> rather than through a system you can rely on."

**How we solve it:**

> "We design an operating layer that defines how work flows. This improves how you make decisions and prioritise
> work. Doing this creates a predictable system, which is what you need to stop firefighting and start building the
> business."

---

## #2 - Profit margins stay flat or decline as revenue grows

**How it shows up:**

- "Revenue's up but profit isn't."
- "Every time we grow, we have to hire more people."
- "More sales just means more complexity."
- "Margins are getting squeezed."
- "We're growing but not more profitable."
- "Costs scale at the same rate as revenue."

**How to say it back:**

> "It sounds like revenue is increasing but costs are increasing at the same rate. You're adding headcount to cope
> with growth, but by paying for more resources to cope with more work, profit margins aren't actually improving."

**How we solve it:**

> "We identify where margin leaks and show you exactly where efficiency is being lost. We then redesign those
> processes so they scale without proportional cost growth, which is what you need to grow profitably."

---

## #3 - Employees use AI to make their own job easier, not to make the business more efficient

**How it shows up:**

- "We use AI chat tools a lot, but I don't think we're getting the full benefit."
- "My people work faster with these tools, but the business hasn't changed."
- "Everyone's found their own shortcuts, but it's not systematic."
- "The team uses AI to get through their work, but we're not actually more productive as a company."
- "Employees have figured out ways to be more efficient, but we're not seeing it at the org level."

**How to say it back:**

> "It sounds like employees are using AI to work faster individually, but the gains aren't being captured at the
> organisational level. Their productivity improves and they're reducing the time it takes to complete tasks, but
> that never gets integrated with the rest of the business - so people end up doing less, but it's not making more
> for the business."

**How we solve it:**

> "We install company-wide AI systems. This embeds AI into workflows and processes so the gains compound across the
> business. Doing this captures the efficiency structurally, which is what you need to actually benefit from AI at the
> business level."

---

## #4 - You're paying for manual work that should be automated or not exist at all

**How it shows up:**

- "Orders come in by email, then we have to enter them into 3-4 different systems."
- "We're copying and pasting between platforms all day."
- "Same information goes into multiple places - very manual."
- "Reports compiled weekly from spreadsheets."
- "We've got one person whose full-time job is just [admin task]."
- "Half my team's day is just data entry."

**How to say it back:**

> "It sounds like you're paying people to manually copy and paste information between systems, instead of doing work
> that adds value."

**How we solve it:**

> "We map the repetitive effort and quantify what it's costing you. This shows you exactly where capacity is being
> wasted. We then automate where possible and eliminate unnecessary steps where automation isn't even needed, which
> is what you need to reclaim that capacity for actual productive work."

---

## #5 - Your company's knowledge and data lives in people's heads, not in systems

**How it shows up:**

- "Decisions depend on specific individuals."
- "When someone leaves, capability drops."
- "Onboarding takes months because so much is undocumented."
- "If [key person] isn't available, we can't answer that."
- "Processes aren't written down anywhere."
- "Institutional memory is all informal."

**How to say it back:**

> "It sounds like decisions depend on specific individuals, and when those people aren't available or leave,
> capability drops."

**How we solve it:**

> "We extract the knowledge from people's heads and codify it into systems, rules, and decision trees. This turns
> expertise into repeatable capability that anyone can use. Doing this removes the dependency on specific people,
> which is what you need to scale without losing capability when key people aren't around."

## Mapping probing questions -> constraints

From `call-script.md`:

- "Has profitability kept pace..." -> **#2**
- "Walk me through a typical day..." -> **#1**
- "Wave a magic wand and double..." -> **whichever surfaces first - the loudest constraint**
- "Have you implemented any AI..." -> **#3**
- "What systems do you use..." -> infrastructure under **#1 and #4**
- "How much of your team's work is prep / admin..." -> **#4** (also: this is where the quantification number comes
  from)
- "When someone needs an answer..." -> **#5**
